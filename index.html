M3RNet: Enhancing Multimodal Emotion Recognition with Memory-Augmented Transformers Wu, T., Huang, Y., & Purwanto, E. (2025) Submitted: IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) 2026

Role: First Author

M3RNet enhances long-term feature retention in multimodal emotion recognition via a memory-augmented Transformer component (Memory Stream). Built on the TMBL framework, it enables advanced multimodal fusion of physiological and video data. Experiments show M3RNet achieves +9.15% accuracy on PPB-Emo and +10.09% over baselines on CL-Drive.

Phy-FusionNet: A Memory-Augmented Transformer for Multimodal Emotion Recognition with Periodicity and Contextual Attention Wu, T., Purwanto, E., & Huang, Y. (2025) Published: IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing

Role: First Author

Phy-FusionNet incorporates a Fourier-based periodicity modeling module to capture intrinsic physiological rhythms and a head hybrid attention mechanism. Validated on WESAD, CL-Drive, and PPB-Emo databases, achieving performance improvements of 13.75% over standard transformers.
